# ClientEngine
## The 7-Touch Follow-Up System

---

## THE SYSTEM OVERVIEW

Most sales require 5-7 touchpoints. Most people give up after 1-2. This system ensures you stay top-of-mind without being annoying.

**The Goal:** Move prospects from "aware" to "interested" to "ready to talk" through strategic, value-based touches.

**The Timeline:** 21 days from first contact to final break-up

---

## THE 7-TOUCH SEQUENCE

### TOUCH 1: Initial Contact (Day 0)
**Channel:** Email or LinkedIn
**Purpose:** Start conversation
**Duration:** Immediate

**The Approach:**
- Personalized opening (show you did research)
- Identify specific pain point
- Soft ask for conversation
- Low friction CTA

**Success Metric:** Response or meeting booked

---

### TOUCH 2: Value Add #1 (Day 3)
**Channel:** Email
**Purpose:** Provide value, stay visible
**Duration:** 2-3 sentences

**The Approach:**
- Reference your first email
- Share relevant resource (article, case study, tool)
- No ask — pure value
- Builds goodwill

**Template:**
```
Hi [NAME],

Came across this [article/case study] on [topic] and immediately thought of you.

[One-sentence summary of why it's relevant]

[LINK]

No pitch — just thought you'd find it useful.

[YOUR_NAME]
```

**Success Metric:** Engagement (click, reply, or continued conversation)

---

### TOUCH 3: Social Proof (Day 7)
**Channel:** Email
**Purpose:** Demonstrate credibility
**Duration:** 3-4 sentences

**The Approach:**
- Reference previous value
- Share client result/case study
- Show relevance to their situation
- Soft ask

**Template:**
```
Hi [NAME],

Wanted to share a quick win: [CLIENT_NAME] in [SIMILAR_INDUSTRY] just [SPECIFIC_RESULT] using the system we discussed.

They were dealing with [SIMILAR_PAIN_POINT] — sound familiar?

Worth a brief conversation to see if this might work for [COMPANY]?

[CALENDAR_LINK]

[YOUR_NAME]
```

**Success Metric:** Meeting booked or positive response

---

### TOUCH 4: Direct Ask (Day 10)
**Channel:** Email
**Purpose:** Clear call to action
**Duration:** 2-3 sentences

**The Approach:**
- Acknowledge silence
- Direct question
- Make it easy to say yes or no

**Template:**
```
Hi [NAME],

I've reached out a couple times about helping [COMPANY] with [PAIN_POINT]. Haven't heard back, so I'm guessing now's not the right time.

Is [PAIN_POINT] something you're looking to solve this quarter, or should I check back later?

Either way is fine — just want to make sure I'm not being annoying!

[YOUR_NAME]
```

**Success Metric:** Direct response (yes, no, or timing)

---

### TOUCH 5: Pattern Interrupt (Day 14)
**Channel:** Different medium (LinkedIn, phone, video)
**Purpose:** Break through noise
**Duration:** 30 seconds (video) or 2 minutes (call)

**The Approach:**
- Use different channel than previous touches
- Short, unexpected format
- Personal and direct

**Video Message Template:**
```
"Hey [NAME], [YOUR_NAME] here. I know you've probably been getting my emails and thinking 'who is this person?' So I wanted to put a face to the name.

Quick 30 seconds: I help [TARGET] [ACHIEVE_OUTCOME]. Recently worked with [SIMILAR_COMPANY] who [RESULT].

If [PAIN_POINT] is something you're dealing with, happy to chat. If not, no worries — just wanted to reach out personally.

[YOUR_NAME]"
```

**Success Metric:** Response or meeting booked

---

### TOUCH 6: The Case Study (Day 17)
**Channel:** Email with attachment
**Purpose:** Deep value + proof
**Duration:** 3-4 sentences + case study

**The Approach:**
- Detailed case study attached
- Show complete transformation
- Relevant to their specific situation
- Final soft pitch

**Template:**
```
Hi [NAME],

I know I've reached out a few times about [PAIN_POINT]. Rather than keep bugging you, I thought I'd share exactly how we helped [SIMILAR_COMPANY] solve this.

Attached is a case study showing:
- Where they started
- The exact system we built
- Results after 90 days

If this resonates, let's talk: [CALENDAR_LINK]

If not, I'll stop reaching out. Either way, hope you find the case study useful.

[YOUR_NAME]

[ATTACH: Case Study PDF]
```

**Success Metric:** Meeting booked or resource engagement

---

### TOUCH 7: The Break-Up (Day 21)
**Channel:** Email
**Purpose:** Close the loop
**Duration:** 2-3 sentences

**The Approach:**
- Acknowledge the silence
- Remove pressure
- Leave door open
- Ask for referral

**Template:**
```
Hi [NAME],

I've reached out several times about helping [COMPANY] with [PAIN_POINT]. Haven't heard back, so I'm assuming now's not the right time or I'm barking up the wrong tree.

Totally fine either way — just want to close the loop rather than keep bothering you.

If [PAIN_POINT] becomes a priority in the future, feel free to reach out: [EMAIL] or [CALENDAR_LINK]

Also — if there's someone else at [COMPANY] I should be talking to, I'd appreciate the pointer.

Best of luck with everything,
[YOUR_NAME]
```

**Success Metric:** Final response or referral

## AUTOMATION SETUP

### CRM Configuration

**Pipeline Stages:**
1. New Lead
2. Contacted (Touch 1 sent)
3. Engaged (responded or opened multiple emails)
4. Meeting Scheduled
5. Proposal Sent
6. Negotiation
7. Closed Won
8. Closed Lost

**Automation Triggers:**

**When Lead Enters "Contacted":**
- Schedule Touch 2 (Day 3)
- Add to "Follow-Up Sequence" tag

**When Lead Enters "Engaged":**
- Pause automated sequence
- Create task for manual follow-up

**When No Response After Touch 4:**
- Tag as "Needs Pattern Interrupt"
- Create task for video/call

**When Lead Enters "Meeting Scheduled":**
- Remove from follow-up sequence
- Add to "Active Opportunity" workflow

### Email Automation Rules

**Rule 1: Opened but No Reply**
- If opened 3+ times but no reply after Touch 3
- Trigger: "Pattern Interrupt" task

**Rule 2: Clicked Link**
- If clicked resource link
- Trigger: Move to Touch 4 immediately

**Rule 3: Replied**
- If any reply received
- Trigger: Pause automation, create manual task

**Rule 4: Bounced**
- If email bounces
- Trigger: Try LinkedIn, then mark as invalid

## MEASUREMENT & OPTIMIZATION

### Key Metrics

| Metric | Target | How to Improve |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| Open Rate | 40%+ | Better subject lines, sender reputation |
| Response Rate | 10%+ | More personalization, better timing |
| Meeting Book Rate | 5%+ | Clearer value proposition, easier CTA |
| Sequence Completion | 100% | Automation monitoring |

### A/B Testing Priorities

1. **Subject Lines** — Biggest impact on open rates
2. **First Sentence** — Determines if they keep reading
3. **CTA** — Button vs. text, specific vs. general
4. **Send Time** — Morning vs. afternoon, day of week
5. **Follow-Up Timing** — 3 days vs. 5 days vs. 7 days

### Monthly Review Checklist

- [ ] Review all sequences for responses
- [ ] Identify common objections
- [ ] Update templates based on what worked
- [ ] Refresh case studies and social proof
- [ ] Remove bounced/invalid emails
- [ ] Tag high-engagement leads for priority follow-up

## ADVANCED TACTICS

### The "P.S." Strategy

Always include a P.S. — it's the second most-read part of an email (after the subject line).

**Use P.S. for:**
- Additional value (link to resource)
- Social proof ("P.S. Just helped [CLIENT] achieve [RESULT]")
- Humor/personality
- Secondary CTA

### The "Re:" Technique

For Touch 3+, use "Re:" in subject line to imply ongoing conversation.

Examples:
- "Re: Quick question about [COMPANY]"
- "Re: [RESOURCE_NAME] I mentioned"

### The Video Touch

Use tools like Loom or Vidyard for personalized video messages.

**When to use video:**
- Touch 5 (pattern interrupt)
- After they open but don't respond to 3+ emails
- For high-value prospects
- When you have specific visual to share

## TROUBLESHOOTING

### "They're not opening emails"

**Check:**
- Subject lines (too salesy?)
- Sender name (personal vs. company)
- Timing (are you sending at 3 AM?)
- Deliverability (are you landing in spam?)

**Fix:**
- Test different subject line styles
- Send from personal email
- Try LinkedIn instead
- Check spam score with Mail-Tester.com

### "They're opening but not responding"

**Check:**
- Is your ask clear?
- Is your value proposition compelling?
- Are you making it easy to respond?

**Fix:**
- Simplify your CTA (one clear action)
- Lead with value, not features
- Make "no" an option (removes pressure)
- Try different channel (phone, video)

### "They responded but went silent"

**Check:**
- Did you respond fast enough?
- Was your follow-up too aggressive?
- Did you answer their question?

**Fix:**
- Respond within 2 hours during business hours
- Match their energy level
- Provide additional value before asking again
