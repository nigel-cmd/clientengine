## THE DISCOVERY CALL FRAMEWORK

**Purpose:** Qualify the prospect, understand their situation, determine fit
**Duration:** 30-45 minutes
**Goal:** Book a strategy session or make an offer

---

## PRE-CALL PREPARATION

### Research Checklist (5 minutes before call)

- [ ] Review their LinkedIn profile
- [ ] Check company website for recent news
- [ ] Look for mutual connections
- [ ] Review any previous email/LinkedIn exchanges
- [ ] Prepare 2-3 personalized questions based on research

### Mindset

- You're interviewing them as much as they're interviewing you
- Your goal is to determine if you can help, not to sell at all costs
- Be curious, not pushy
- Take notes — you'll need them for follow-up

---

## THE CALL STRUCTURE

### OPENING (5 minutes)

**Build Rapport:**

"Thanks for taking the time today, [NAME]. Before we dive in, how's your week going?"

[Listen, respond naturally, find common ground]

**Set Agenda:**

"Here's what I'd love to cover in the next 30 minutes:

1. First, I want to understand your current situation — what's working, what's not
2. Then we can talk about what you're looking to achieve
3. Finally, I'll share how we help and we can see if it makes sense to work together

Sound good? What would be most valuable for you to cover?"

**Get Permission:**

"Great. Do you mind if I take some notes? I want to make sure I capture everything."

---

### DISCOVERY QUESTIONS (15-20 minutes)

**Current Situation:**

1. "Walk me through how you're currently getting clients. What's working?"

2. "Where do most of your clients come from right now?"

3. "What's your biggest challenge with client acquisition?"

**Pain Points:**

4. "How is that affecting your business? Your stress level?"

5. "What have you already tried to solve this? What happened?"

6. "What happens if you don't solve this in the next 90 days?"

**Desired Outcome:**

7. "If we were having this conversation 90 days from now, what would have changed for you to consider this a success?"

8. "How many new clients would you need to hit your goals?"

9. "What would it mean for your business if you had a predictable client acquisition system?"

**Decision Process:**

10. "Who else is involved in this decision?"

11. "What does your timeline look like for solving this?"

12. "Have you budgeted for solving this, or is this a new priority?"

---

### THE TRANSITION (2 minutes)

**Summarize What You Heard:**

"Let me make sure I understand. You're currently [CURRENT SITUATION], which is causing [PAIN POINT]. You've tried [PREVIOUS ATTEMPTS] but [WHY THEY DIDN'T WORK].

If you could [DESIRED OUTCOME] in the next 90 days, that would be a game-changer because [WHY IT MATTERS].

Did I get that right? What did I miss?"

**Get Agreement:**

"Okay, so if we could solve [PAIN POINT] and get you to [DESIRED OUTCOME], that would be worth exploring?"

---

### THE PITCH (5-7 minutes)

**Transition to Solution:**

"Here's how we help [TARGET DESCRIPTION] like you [ACHIEVE OUTCOME].

We build you a complete client acquisition system in 30 days. That means:

1. **We research your ideal clients** — exactly who they are, where to find them, what they care about

2. **We build your outreach sequences** — every email, every LinkedIn message, written in your voice, designed to start conversations

3. **We set up your follow-up automation** — 7-touch nurture sequences that work while you sleep

4. **We connect your calendar** — qualified prospects book directly, you just show up and close

5. **We give you the scripts and training** — so you know exactly what to say on calls

By day 30, you have a complete system running. Not theory. Not templates. A working engine that generates appointments."

**Social Proof:**

"We just did this for [SIMILAR_CLIENT] in [INDUSTRY]. They were [SIMILAR_SITUATION]. Within [TIMEFRAME], they [SPECIFIC_RESULT]."

**The Offer:**

"The investment for ClientEngine is $697. That includes everything I just mentioned — the research, the sequences, the automation setup, the scripts, and 30 days of support.

Most of our clients make that back with their first new client."

### HANDLING OBJECTIONS

#### "I need to think about it"

**Response:**

"Totally understand. What specifically do you need to think through? Is it [PRICE/TIMING/FIT/OTHER]?

[Listen]

Got it. Here's what I'd suggest — let's schedule a time to talk [SPECIFIC TIMEFRAME]. That gives you time to [WHAT THEY NEED], and I can answer any questions that come up.

Would [DAY] or [DAY] work better?"

---

#### "It's too expensive"

**Response:**

"I hear that. Let me ask — what's it costing you to NOT have a consistent client acquisition system?

[Let them answer]

Right. So if this helps you land just one client at your current rate, it pays for itself. Most of our clients see ROI within the first 30 days.

Is the concern the price, or is it whether this will actually work for your situation?"

---

#### "I need to talk to my partner/spouse"

**Response:**

"Absolutely, that makes sense. What questions do you think they'll have?

[Listen]

Great. Would it help if I sent you a brief summary of what we discussed that you can share with them? And maybe we can schedule a call with both of you?

When would be a good time to follow up?"

---

#### "I'm already working with someone"

**Response:**

"Got it. How's that going? Are you getting the results you wanted?

[Listen]

I see. What would need to change for you to be 100% satisfied with your current setup?

[Listen]

Interesting. Here's what I'd suggest — let's stay in touch. If things change or you want to compare approaches, I'm happy to show you how we do things.

Can I check back in [TIMEFRAME]?"

---

#### "I don't have time right now"

**Response:**

"I totally get that. Can I ask — when will you have time? Is this a priority that's just delayed, or is it not a priority right now?

[Listen]

Okay. Here's the thing — the system we build runs without you. That's the whole point. You invest 30 days upfront, then it works while you focus on delivery.

But if now's really not the right time, I understand. When would be better to revisit this?"

---

### CLOSING THE CALL (3-5 minutes)

**If They're Ready to Buy:**

"Great! Here's what happens next:

1. I'll send you the onboarding questionnaire — takes about 10 minutes
2. We'll schedule your kickoff call for [TIMEFRAME]
3. We start building your system immediately

I'll send you the payment link and the questionnaire right after this call. Any questions before we wrap up?"

**If They Need Time:**

"No problem at all. Here's what I'll do:

1. I'll send you a summary of what we discussed
2. I'll include a link to book your next call when you're ready
3. If you have any questions in the meantime, just reply to my email

What would be a good time for me to follow up?"

**If They're Not a Fit:**

"You know what, based on what you've shared, I don't think we're the right fit right now. [EXPLAIN WHY]

But here's what I'd recommend instead: [ALTERNATIVE SOLUTION/REFERRAL]

Can I check back in [TIMEFRAME] to see how things are going?"

## ADVANCED TECHNIQUES

### The "Prescription Close"

Instead of asking "Do you want to buy?" say:

"Based on what you've shared, here's what I recommend: We build you the complete ClientEngine system. That gives you [SPECIFIC BENEFIT] within [TIMEFRAME].

Does that sound like the right approach for your situation?"

### The "Alternative Close"

"Would you prefer to start with the full system, or would the payment plan work better for your cash flow?"

[Either way, they buy]

### The "Assumptive Close"

"What day works better for your kickoff call — Tuesday or Thursday?"

[Assumes they're buying, focuses on logistics]

## PRACTICE DRILLS

### Drill 1: The 60-Second Pitch

Time yourself. Can you explain what you do and who you help in 60 seconds or less?

### Drill 2: Objection Roulette

Write common objections on index cards. Pull one at random and practice your response.

### Drill 3: Role Play

Have someone play a difficult prospect. Practice staying calm and guiding the conversation.

### Drill 4: Record Yourself

Record a practice call. Listen for:
- Filler words (um, uh, like)
- Talking too fast
- Not letting the prospect finish
- Missing follow-up questions

## REMEMBER

- **Listen more than you talk** — 70/30 rule (them/you)
- **Ask questions before making statements**
- **Summarize before pitching** — shows you listened
- **Be okay with "no"** — not everyone is your client
- **Follow up fast** — within 1 hour while you're top of mind
